Curve Interpretation

I-V Curve Analysis

The I-V curve shows the relationship between current and voltage in a solar cell. Key points to observe:

  • Short Circuit Current (Isc): Maximum current when voltage is zero
  • Open Circuit Voltage (Voc): Maximum voltage when current is zero
  • Maximum Power Point (MPP): The point where the product of current and voltage is maximum

P-V Curve Analysis

The P-V curve shows how power output varies with voltage. Important characteristics:

  • The curve peaks at the Maximum Power Point (MPP)
  • The fill factor (FF) indicates the "squareness" of the I-V curve
  • Higher fill factors indicate better performance

Performance Indicators

  • Fill Factor (FF): Ratio of maximum power to the product of Voc and Isc. Higher is better (typically 0.7-0.85).
  • Efficiency: Ratio of maximum power output to input solar power.
  • Series Resistance (Rs): Lower values reduce power losses.
  • Shunt Resistance (Rsh): Higher values reduce leakage currents.
